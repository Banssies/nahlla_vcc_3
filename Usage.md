## ✨ Potential Future Use Cases

Aether could one day support people in situations like:

- struggling to begin a task
- forgetting self-care during stress
- feeling overwhelmed by large responsibilities
- needing a calm prompt instead of an aggressive reminder
- wanting structure without feeling controlled

---

## 🌿 Example Scenarios

### Gentle task support
A user is overwhelmed by a long to-do list.  
Aether suggests beginning with one tiny step instead of focusing on the whole list.

### Self-care prompting
A user has been working for hours without a break.  
Aether offers a kind reminder to pause, hydrate, or breathe.

### Emotional interruption support
A user appears frozen or avoidant.  
Aether reframes progress into something smaller and more approachable.

### Soft recovery after missed tasks
Rather than focusing on failure, Aether helps the user re-enter their routine without shame.

## 🌙 Intended Audience

Aether may be especially meaningful for people who experience:

- executive dysfunction
- burnout
- overwhelm
- emotional exhaustion
- difficulty starting tasks
- a need for softer support systems

This does not limit who it is for, but it helps explain the kind of care-centered design it prioritizes.
